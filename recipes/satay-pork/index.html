Pork Satay

Skewered BBQ with peanut dipping sauce

********************

Seasoning Ingredients

  1. Grind finely.

Sauce

  1. Fry groundnuts on low heat without oil until brown, stirring continuously. Cool, sskin and pound coarsely.
  2. Grind corriander and cummin seeds finely.
  3. Grind shallots, garlic, lemon grass and tumeric finely.
  4. Add shrimp and tamarind paste and mix well.
  5. Grind chillies finely.
  6. Add 2 rice bowls of water to grated coconut to sqeueeze for milk.

Ketupat (rice cakes)

  1. Pack rice in cloth bags and boiling til cooked. Remove from bags and cube.

Pork Satay

  1. Heat pan, add 10 tablespoonfuls cooking oil. When oil is hot, add all ground ingredients and fry until fragrant, add a little coconut milk while stirring so that the mixture will not burn or dry up.
  2. Add remaining coconut milk, sugar and salt to taste and bring to boil. The sauce should be slightly on the sweet side.
  3. Add pounded groundnuts, stir to mix well. Dish out.
  4. Barbequeue meat over grill or charcoal fire. See that the charcoal is red hot before grilling. If not, the satay will have a smoky taste. Turn over often to prevent burning. Skewer strips of fat between lean meat.
  5. Remove when meat is cooked.
  6. Serve with sauce, cut cucmber slices and raw onions.

Seasoning Ingredients

  • 6 shallots
  • 4 pips garlic
  • 1 tsp ground ketumbar
  • 1/4 tsp ground jintan putih
  • thumb-sized fresh kunyit

Sauce

  • 150 g groundnuts
  • 5 dried chillies soaked in warm water for 15 minutes
  • 5 shallots
  • 4 pips garlic
  • 1 stalk serai
  • thumbsized fresh kunyit
  • 3 tbsp ketumbar
  • 1 1/2 tsp jintan putih
  • 1/2 coconut, grated
  • 1 tsp belacan
  • sugar and salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste

Ketupat (rice cakes)

  • rice

Pork Satay

  • Seasoning Ingredients
  • Sauce
  • Ketupat (rice cakes)
  • 600 g pork, preferrably streaky
  • 40 lidi sticks
